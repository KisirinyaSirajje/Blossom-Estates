Rental Guide

Renting in Makerere: What You Need to Know

Whether you're a student, professional, or family looking for a rental property in Makerere, our comprehensive guide will help you navigate the rental process with ease.

1

Determine Your Budget

Consider all costs including rent, utilities, security deposit, and potential maintenance fees. As a general rule, your rent should not exceed 30% of your monthly income.

2

Choose the Right Location

Consider proximity to your workplace or university, access to public transportation, safety of the neighborhood, and nearby amenities like shops and restaurants.

3

Property Viewing

Schedule viewings for multiple properties to compare options. Check for proper maintenance, security features, water supply, and electrical systems during your visit.

4

Understand the Lease Agreement

Carefully review the lease terms including rent amount, payment schedule, lease duration, security deposit, maintenance responsibilities, and termination conditions.

5

Document Property Condition

Before moving in, document the condition of the property with photos and a written inventory to avoid disputes when moving out.

Frequently Asked Questions

What documents do I need to rent a property?

Typically, you'll need identification documents (national ID or passport), proof of income (employment letter or bank statements), references from previous landlords, and sometimes a guarantor depending on the property value.

How much is the security deposit?

Security deposits in Makerere typically range from one to three months' rent, depending on the property type and landlord requirements. This deposit is refundable at the end of your lease, subject to property condition.

Are utilities included in the rent?

This varies by property. Some rentals include certain utilities like water or security services, while others require tenants to pay for all utilities separately. Each property listing specifies what is included in the rent.

Can I make modifications to the rental property?

Minor decorative changes like hanging pictures are usually permitted, but significant modifications require written permission from the landlord. Always check your lease agreement and consult with your property manager before making changes.

What is the typical lease duration?

Most residential leases in Makerere are for 12 months, though some landlords offer 6-month or 24-month options. Short-term rentals (1-3 months) are also available but typically at a premium rate.
